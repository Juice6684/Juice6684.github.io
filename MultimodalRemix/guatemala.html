Guatemala

Mr. Estrada was born into the war-torn country of Guatemala, a small country sitting south of Mexico. The Guatemalan Civil War just began, a bloody conflict spanning many decades.

The war was a widespread event that affected every part of the nation, causing mass poverty and displacement among communities.

A mass genecide of the indiginous people there was sponsored and orchestrated by the US government to help fufill the interests of the United Fruit Company, who saw the indiginous people as a roadblock against their ability to buy large amounts of land.

Local residents watch as Guatemalan army soldiers show captured banners made by militant guerrilla group Ejército Guerrillero de los Pobres, EGP, in Huehuetenango, Guatemala, October 1, 1982. (Robert Nickelsberg)

His father was a part of the EGP, a far-leftist group seeking to overthrow the current fascist government.He would attend secret meetings, hosted by the party, and plan out strategies and actions to support their cause. He would distribute underground newspapers and posters around the area filled with far left, libertarian content inspired by the ideaoligies of Che Guevara
